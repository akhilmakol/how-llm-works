Large language models learn patterns from text. A transformer reads tokens, builds embeddings, applies attention, and predicts the next token. Attention helps each token focus on the most relevant earlier tokens in the sequence. During training, the model compares its predictions with the true next words and updates its weights. During inference, the model generates text one token at a time. This tiny project explains tokenization, embeddings, attention, transformer blocks, training, and inference in a simple way. Learning the small version makes the big ideas easier to understand.
