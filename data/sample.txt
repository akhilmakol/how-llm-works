Banks collect deposits, extend loans, manage liquidity, and earn interest income. A transformer reads tokens, builds embeddings, applies attention, and predicts the next token. In a banking scenario, attention can help the model connect terms such as borrower, collateral, credit risk, capital ratio, and net interest margin. During training, the model compares its predictions with the true next words and updates its weights. During inference, the model generates text one token at a time. This tiny project explains tokenization, embeddings, attention, transformer blocks, training, and inference in a simple way using banking fundamentals as the running theme. Learning the small version makes the big ideas easier to understand.
